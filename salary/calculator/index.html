How This Calculator Works

We combine salary data from three dimensions (seniority, location, and company stage) to estimate your market rate. Each dimension contributes to the final range based on relative weighting from the State of GTM Engineering Report 2026 (228 respondents + 3,342 job postings).

For detailed methodology, see our data methodology page.

Understanding Your Salary Estimate

The range this calculator produces reflects where you'd fall in the current market. Seniority drives 40% of the estimate, location 35%, and company stage 25%. These weights come from regression analysis of actual job postings and survey responses.

Your actual offer may vary based on factors this calculator doesn't capture: specific technical skills (engineers who code earn 15-25% more, per our coding premium analysis), industry vertical (fintech and cybersecurity pay above market), and negotiation skill.

Key Factors That Shift Your Range

Coding ability: Python and SQL proficiency is the single largest salary differentiator for GTM Engineers. Engineers who can write code, build API integrations, and create custom Clay actions earn at the top of every range this calculator produces.

Company stage trade-offs: Seed-stage companies offer lower base salaries but larger equity grants. Enterprise companies pay the highest base salaries with smaller equity. Growth-stage companies often offer the best total compensation when you factor in both base and equity.

Location adjustments: Remote roles have narrowed the geographic salary gap, but in-office roles in SF and NYC still pay 15-25% premiums. Our US vs global comparison shows US-based engineers earn significantly more than international counterparts.

Bonus structure: Most GTM Engineers receive performance bonuses tied to pipeline metrics. Bonus data shows bonuses typically add 10-20% on top of base salary, with variation by company stage.

Negotiation Tips Based on Your Estimate

When you have your range, use it as a starting point in negotiations. Lead with your pipeline impact: contacts enriched, meetings booked, revenue influenced. Hiring managers respond to concrete ROI numbers. Check the career guide for more negotiation strategies tailored to your seniority level.

The 205% year-over-year growth in GTM Engineer job postings means talent supply is tight. If your skills match the role requirements, the negotiating power is yours. Companies report 2-3x longer time-to-fill for GTM Engineer positions compared to adjacent roles.
